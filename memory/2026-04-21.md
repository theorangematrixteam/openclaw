## Outreach Email Framework (researched 2026 best practices)
- **Format:** 50-125 words, short and casual
- **Structure:** Trigger (specific observation) → Problem → Solution → Proof → Ask
- **Subject line:** 2-6 words, casual, lowercase, under 40 chars
- **Tone:** Friend, not seller. "Vibe with it" not "schedule a demo"
- **Offer:** Free sample reel — zero risk, hard to refuse
- **One-stop solution pitch:** One team handles everything so founder can focus on product + growth
- **Follow-up sequence:** Day 1 → Day 4 → Day 9 → Day 14 (breakup)
- **Best times:** Tue-Thu, 9-11 AM or 1-3 PM IST
- **Personalization:** Reference specific product/category/drop, not just name
- **Response rate benchmarks:** Generic 1%, personalized 5-8%, video 15-35%

## Outreach Psychology Principles (from 48 Laws of Power + research)
**Always use these for client retention and lead conversion:**

1. **Law 13 — Ask for nothing first.** Give before asking. Free sample reel = reciprocity trigger.
2. **Law 16 — Use absence to increase respect.** Don't chase. "Still rooting for you" = we're fine either way.
3. **Law 30 — Make accomplishments seem effortless.** Don't oversell. "One team" not "we have 15 specialists."
4. **Never criticize the prospect's current state.** Don't say "your social is weak." Say "drops are sick" instead.
5. **Friend tone, not seller tone.** Talk like you're hyping up a friend's brand, not pitching a service.
6. **Remove all pressure.** "No strings at all. If it resonates, cool. If not, still rooting for you."
7. **Show, don't tell.** Portfolio link speaks louder than claims. Sample reel proves value before asking for money.
8. **One-stop solution = peace of mind.** Frame it as removing a burden, not adding a service.
9. **Curiosity over conclusion.** "Let us know?" not "Schedule a call." Lower friction.
10. **Specificity builds trust.** Reference their actual products/drops, not generic compliments.
