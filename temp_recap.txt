Notch India — April 2026 Recap

Prepared by Orange Matrix


Where we are right now

April was the launch month. 17 pieces planned across Instagram — a mix of reels, carousels, and posts. The goal was simple: get the brand visible and start building recognition before the full collection drops.

10 pieces are approved and ready to schedule. 5 are waiting on the client for assets or sign-off. 2 more are in creation. That's a solid pipeline, but the 5 pending items are the bottleneck — nothing moves to scheduling without approval.


What we shipped

Soft launch content — the "coming soon" reel and aesthetic product shots are designed to create curiosity, not sell. The idea is to make people stop and wonder what this brand is, not tell them.

Visual grid series — 9 product photos planned as a consistent grid. This gives the profile a coherent look when someone visits, which matters because Instagram's algorithm favors profiles that look intentional.

Trend-led reels — Earth Day reel ties the brand to a cultural moment. The "Why Notch India" reel starts shifting from "what is this" to "why should I care," which is the transition from awareness to consideration.

UGC/reality content — one reel featuring a real person wearing the product, focused on age inclusivity. This is where the brand starts feeling human instead of polished.


What's working

The content buckets (Product, Trends, UGC) are clearly defined, which means next month's planning is faster because the structure exists.

The carousel and reel split matches what the algorithm rewards — carousels for saves and dwell time, reels for reach to new audiences.

The hook strategy for reels uses the 3-second rule — visual changes and curiosity gaps in the opening to stop the scroll.


What needs to move

The 5 items pending client sign-off are the priority. Until those move, we can't schedule the full month's content. The shared sheet is the approval tool — the client marks status directly.

Once approval crosses 80%, we shift the content strategy from "who is Notch India" to "why you need Notch India." That's the transition from awareness to consideration, and it changes the hooks, CTAs, and content angles.


May direction

Two shifts for next month:

1. From brand-led to community-led. More real people, less polished product shots. The UGC reel this month is the prototype — May should have 3-4 of these. Trust comes from seeing real people, not logos.

2. Seasonal relevance. Summer content angles — lightweight fabrics, breathable designs, hot weather styling. This gives the algorithm timely hooks and gives people a reason to engage now instead of later.


Action items

- Get the 5 pending posts approved via the shared sheet
- Start sourcing UGC content from real customers or models
- Plan May content calendar by April 25
- Prepare seasonal content hooks (summer angles)
